## Project Overview

This project is a part of the Digital Literacy course (CSE0001) which is currently in process at VIT Bhopal University. The aim of this project is to learn how we can effectively use digital tools, build our professional presence, and stay safe online.

I am a Student Digital Ambassador, and I have tried to learn various activities that students can be made aware of to become responsible citizens in the digital world. This project is divided into five modules.
---

## Module Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic (Module 1)

Created a one-page visual infographic with the help of **Canva**, which helps my batchmates grasp the idea of what Digital Literacy is and why it is important. The infographic includes three important topics:
- What digital literacy is and the definition of the term
- Digital tools that can be useful for students
- Professional online presence: tips and advice on creating an online presence
### Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio (Module 2)

Set up professional digital profiles on **GitHub**, **LinkedIn**, and **Kaggle**. Each platform serves a unique purpose:
- **GitHub**  — for code, projects, and a profile README showcasing my interests in Machine Learning, Health Tech & Innovation
- **LinkedIn** — for professional networking, internship opportunities, and academic connections (Education section filled with B.Tech, VIT Bhopal, Sep 2025 – Sep 2029)
- **Kaggle**  — for data science practice and competitions

### Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette Guide (Module 4)

Drafted two professional emails:
1. **Email to Professor** — requesting an assignment deadline extension, following all professional email conventions (clear subject line, proper greeting, structured body, formal sign-off)
2. **Email to Internship Coordinator** — expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity, highlighting skills and motivation

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study (Module 5)

Researched **UPI / Online Payment Fraud** and wrote a detailed case study covering how it happens (social engineering, fake collect requests, screen-sharing app exploitation), who is targeted (college students, online sellers, digital payment users), and the consequences (financial loss, data compromise, mental distress).

Created a **'Stay Safe Online'** prevention checklist with actionable tips specifically for college students in India, including UPI safety rules and reporting channels:
