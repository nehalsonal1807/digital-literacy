# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

##  DO's

 #  Do 

1 **Think before you post**  Think about whether the information you are about to post projects the image you would like to convey to the world in relation to your professionalism and personality. 
2 **Keep your privacy settings updated**  Always be mindful of who can access your information, such as your posts, photos, and other information. 
 3**Give credit where it's due** Always make sure to give credit to the creators of the information, quotes, and photos you use to avoid plagiarism. 
4**Be respectful in disagreements** Always be willing to engage in a healthy debate, even if you are in disagreement with someone, and make sure to be respectful in your argument. 
5  **Maintain a professional online presence** Remember that your profile is not only visible to your friends but also to your potential employer or professor, so make sure to be professional. 
6  **Verify information before sharing**  Always make sure to verify the information you are about to share with other people, especially news and statistics.


##  DON'Ts

#Don't
1 **Don't overshare personal information** It is not a good idea to post your address, phone number, daily routine, and financial information on social media sites. 
2 **Don't engage in cyberbullying or hate speech** It is not a good idea to post information that demeans, threatens, and harasses people and groups of people. 
3 **Don't post under the influence** It is not a good idea to post information while you are emotionally unstable, angry, or drunk, as you may not remember or delete the information that you have posted. |
4 **Don't plagiarise or misrepresent others' work**  It is not a good idea to share someone else’s information and claim that it is yours, as this may lead to severe academic and legal consequences. 
5 **Don't ignore the privacy of others**  It is not a good idea to tag people in your posts and photographs without seeking permission from them, especially in awkward situations. 
6 **Don't use social media to vent about professors, classmates, or employers**  It is not a good idea to vent your frustrations and upsets in social media, as this may affect your relationships and reputation forever.
